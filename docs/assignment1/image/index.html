2. Image Size Distribution & Aspect Ratio

Remark
  • Image dimensions vary, but within practical limits — Width and height are spread across a moderate range, with many samples near common resize boundaries.
  • Aspect ratio distribution is concentrated — Most images fall near common photo ratios (roughly around 1.0 to 1.5).
  • File size is right-skewed — Most samples are small-to-medium files.
  • Breed-level coverage appears balanced at a high level — Top breeds show similar image counts.

3. Color Space Analysis & Quality Metrics

Remark
  • RGB channel behavior is stable and natural — Mean channel values stay in realistic ranges.
  • Brightness and contrast are centered with moderate spread — Brightness and contrast histograms are unimodal.
  • Sharpness has a heavy tail — Most images have moderate sharpness, with fewer very high-sharpness outliers.
  • Overall data quality profile is suitable for classification pipelines.

Part 2: Detection EDA Report

Bounding boxes + spatial distribution + quality metrics

1. Bounding box properties

Remark
  • Bounding boxes are moderate in size and fairly stable. Mean width is 256.7 px and mean height is 231.9 px.
  • Aspect ratios are mostly landscape. The dataset has 53.7% landscape, 30.4% portrait, and 15.9% square boxes.
  • Area distribution is right-skewed. Most boxes lie in the low-to-mid area range.
  • Size categories separate cleanly in width-height space.

2. Spatial Distribution Analysis

Remark
  • Strong center bias is present — Most boxes cluster around the middle of the image.
  • Normalized area distribution is concentrated — Many boxes have moderate normalized area.
  • There is still positional diversity — A visible long tail indicates off-center samples exist.

Part 3: Segmentation EDA Report

Pixel / shape / boundary + segmentation quality

1. Pixel Analysis

Remark
  • Background overwhelmingly dominates at ~83.63%, meaning birds occupy only a small fraction of each image on average.
  • Foreground (bird body) accounts for ~10.58% and boundary (transition region) for ~5.80% of image pixels.
  • The relatively low standard deviations indicate this pattern is consistent across all 200 breeds.

2. Shape Analysis

Remark
  • Convexity mean of 0.739 shows bird silhouettes are moderately convex.
  • Compactness mean of 0.454 confirms that bird shapes are far from circular.
  • Eccentricity mean of 0.833 further reinforces that most bird masks are significantly elongated.

3. Mask Analysis (Boundary)

Remark
  • Average boundary thickness of 5.92 px indicates a moderate transition zone between foreground and background.
  • Smoothness mean of 0.641 shows boundaries are reasonably smooth but not perfectly clean.
  • Complexity mean of 1046.88 is notably high, reflecting detailed and irregular outlines typical of bird silhouettes.

4. Quality Analysis

Remark
  • Very low avg_coverage (0.164) confirms that birds typically occupy only ~16.4% of the image area.
  • High coverage_cv (0.505) reveals significant within-breed variation in object scale.
  • High fg_cv (0.616) with only 27/200 breeds below 0.50 shows considerable intra-breed variation.

Part 4: Classification EDA Report

Class distribution + split + embeddings

1. Class Distribution

Remark
  • The class histogram is fairly even, and the max/min gap is limited compared with many fine-grained datasets.
  • This balance is a good signal for stable classifier training and more reliable macro-level metrics.

2. Train/Val/Test Distribution

Remark
  • Keep validation/test split stable to compare experiments fairly.
  • If any split is too small, metrics may fluctuate strongly across runs.

3. Embedding Analysis

Remark
  • The t-SNE/UMAP structure shows several local clusters but still noticeable overlap between similar bird breeds.
  • Feature separability is present but fine-grained confusion is expected for visually close species.
  • Better backbone features or metric-learning objectives could improve inter-breed margins.
