💡 10. Key Insights

  1. Content dominance: Movies represent ~70% of the dataset.
  2. Data quality: Significant missing data in director.
  3. Recency: Most content was released after 2015, with outliers representing older classics.
  4. Ratings: TV-MA is a frequent rating, targeted toward mature audiences.
  5. Production: The USA is a leading country in content production.

1. Content Strategy & Distribution

  • Movies Dominate: Netflix’s catalog is heavily skewed toward Movies, which make up nearly 70% of the platform's offerings, compared to TV Shows at 30%.
  • Top Producing Nations: The United States is the undisputed leader in content volume, followed by India and the United Kingdom.
  • Regional Content Preferences: While the US produces a balanced mix, countries like India and Hong Kong are overwhelmingly focused on Movies (>90%). Conversely, countries like South Korea and Japan show a much stronger emphasis on TV Shows compared to the global average.

2. Temporal & Release Trends

  • Recent Growth Spurt: The vast majority of the content currently available was released in the last decade (post-2010), reflecting Netflix's aggressive acquisition and original content production strategy in recent years.
  • Outliers in Antiquity: While the focus is heavily modern, there are notable outliers dating back to the 1920s through the 1970s, indicating a curated but small selection of classic cinema.

3. Audience Targeting (Ratings)

  • Mature Audience Focus: The most frequent content ratings are TV-MA and TV-14, indicating that Netflix strongly targets adult and mature teen demographics rather than primarily focusing on children’s or family-friendly content.

4. Data Quality & Imbalance

  • Missing Information: Fields like director, cast, and country contain meaningful proportions of missing data. Any future ML models or recommendation systems relying on these fields must incorporate robust imputation strategies.
  • Dataset Balance: The 70/30 split between Movies and TV Shows presents a mild to moderate class imbalance. If predictive modeling is the next step, techniques like stratified sampling or class weighting may be necessary.

Dataset characteristics (summary)

  • Dataset contains 8,807 samples with 12 features.
  • Imbalanced dataset: Movie (69.6%) vs TV Show (30.4%) - ratio ~2.3:1.
  • Found missing values in 6 columns:
    • director: 2,634 missing (29.91%)
    • country: 831 missing (9.44%)
    • cast: 825 missing (9.37%)
    • date_added: 10 missing (0.11%)
    • rating: 4 missing (0.05%)
    • duration: 3 missing (0.03%)
  • Suggestion: Use mode/median imputation for columns with <1% missing, and NLP/constant techniques for director and cast.
  • Detected 719 outliers across release_year (8.16%). Suggest investigating these outliers — consider clipping or categorization for classic movies.
  • No high numerical correlations found — features are primarily categorical and relatively independent.
