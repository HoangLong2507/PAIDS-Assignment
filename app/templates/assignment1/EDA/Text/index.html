Key Insights

Dataset Characteristics

  • Slightly Imbalanced Dataset: Categories are slightly imbalanced (Not spam: ~68.7%, Spam: ~31.3%).
  • Email Length: Average 137 words and 843 characters per email (all raw words included).
  • Vocabulary Richness: Spam emails have a relatively smaller vocabulary (963 unique words) compared to Not Spam emails (1861 unique words).
  • Stop Words Density: Stop words make up a significant portion of the text corpus at 40.8% (4,635 out of 11,356 total words).

Data Processing

  • Raw Statistics: Word and character counts include ALL words, including stop words like "the", "to", "and", "you", "your".
  • Vocabulary Analysis: Word frequency and unique vocabulary stats are calculated AFTER removing common stop words.
  • Distinctive Vocabulary: Spam features promotional language (click, win, free, today, just), whereas Not Spam features professional or administrative language (email, company, account, service, privacy).
  • Why TF-IDF & Mixed Approach: Length stats provide real email size, while TF-IDF metrics highlight terms that are contextually unique to Spam or Ham instead of generic high-frequency terms.
